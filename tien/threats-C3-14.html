Although turtles have been around for millions of years, recent human activities have started to threaten the turtle population.

One of the major threats is plastic pollution. When humans don't throw away their plastic waste responsibily, and it blows into the ocean many sea creatures and birds can get caught up in the plastic which can lead to strangulation or starvation. However, turtles are affected especially, as many sea turtles rely on jellyfish as their main source of food. The turtles often mistake plastic bags and other pieces of plastic for the jellyfish, which leads to many of them dying due to plastic pollution. Some sources even suggest that over 1000 turtles are killed each year from eating plastic.

Another reason for why turtles are becoming more and more endangered is global warming. Certain human activities like burning fossil fuels, using non-renewable energy and wasting electricty/water releases greenhouse gases that trap the Earth's heat in the atmosphere, causing it to get warmer. This leads to warmer sea temperatures and this can cause turtles to die because the temperature is too hot.

However, as more people are being educated on the threats, more is also being done. Here are just a few examples:

You can adopt a turtle. This ensures that it is looked after until ready to be released back into the wild, as it is being given enough money to be properly cared for.

Plant some trees. Trees absorb carbon dioxide, a greenhouse gas, and this will reduce the effects of global warming, which will then in turn also lower sea temperatures.
