There are thought to be 356 species of turtles! Today, they are divided into two groups: the Pleurodira and Cryptodira.
The Pleurodira are more commonly known as side-necked turtles, and they are only limited to freshwater habitats in the Southern Hemisphere. The Pleurodira can be predominantly found in Australia, South America and Africa. Their other name, side-necked turtles, comes from the fact that turtle species in this category lay their head on the side, under their shell when they feel threatened.

Some examples of Pleurodira species include:

African Sideneck Turtle

Red-footed Sideneck Turtle

On the other hand, the Cryptodira, also known as hidden-necked turtles, retract their head into their shell as a defensive position. Cryptodires evolved after the Pleurodires, in the Jurassic period, when dinosaurs were alive. Also, they became completely dominant of nost rivers and lakes, and adapted to live on land, making some of them semi-aqautic reptiles.

Some examples of Cryptodira species include:

Snapping Turtle

Western Hermann's Turtle

Green Sea Turtle
