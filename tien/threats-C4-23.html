Although turtles have been around for millions of years, recent human activities have started to threaten the turtle population.

One of the major threats is plastic pollution. When humans don't throw away their plastic waste responsibily, and it blows into the ocean many sea creatures and birds can get caught up in the plastic which can lead to strangulation or starvation. However, turtles are affected especially, as many sea turtles rely on jellyfish as their main source of food. The turtles often mistake plastic bags and other pieces of plastic for the jellyfish, which leads to many of them dying due to plastic pollution. Some sources even suggest that over 1000 turtles are killed each year from eating plastic.

Another cause that has lead to turtle numbers declining is global warming.
